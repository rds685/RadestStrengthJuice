About
Growing up on farm, I was surrounded by natural foods coming from the planted fruits and vegetables grown on the huge farmland. Everything was vibrant and full of color. My family known to eat good from “soul food” to “good cooking.” Some mornings, we would wake up to the sweet aroma of the harvest greens with turkey necks brewing on the fire stove. We’d also get our protein from the cows, pigs, and chickens that were raised on the farm. My family were big hunters as well, so they’d go out, hunt, and we’d eat more deer, rabbit, and turkey. There was no layout of what “eating healthy” meant. As time passed, I’d watched my family, for many years, struggle with obesity. Many suffered heart attacks, strokes, diabetes, high blood pressure, all while steadily filling their pill boxes full of medications for high cholesterol only to counteract with reverse affects from the high blood pressure medications. As I grew older, I’d find myself participating in sports to stay active. I loved everything about healthy eating and staying active. It truly allowed me to feel good about myself. Don’t get me wrong, the pork chops smothered in brown gravy tasted amazing but for some reason, after eating, I would always feel tired, sluggish, and lazy. I continued my journey through college playing basketball and earning a degree in Exercise Science. My love for wellness grew. As a result, juicing became an alternative to health as I discovered an easy, fast, and fun way to consume fruits and vegetables in order absorb essential nutrients in the body that I was not getting on a daily basis. I intend to share this experience with others in a way that I may teach, motivate, and coach people to achieve their fitness goals and improve their lifestyle.
